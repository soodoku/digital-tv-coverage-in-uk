How do I retune? Click to expand

Basic retuning

Retuning should only take a few minutes and may be required when new services are launched or there are technical changes to TV signals in your area.

  • Step-by-step guide to basic retuning

Manual retuning

If you have missing channels after having done a basic retune, or the wrong regional services, you may need to do a manual retune to select where services appear on the channel list (EPG). You can do this using the instructions below.

Your manual retune instructions for today

These instructions assume you are using the Durris transmitter. If you know you get your signal from a different transmitter, click here.

  1. Using your remote control, press the 'menu' button.
  2. Select the 'set up' or 'installation' option. If you see picture icons, select the tool box, satellite dish or spanner.
  3. Select 'manual retune' or 'manual search'.
  4. If you are prompted for a code, try 0000 or 1234.
  5. For BBC A type in 28 where it asks for a 'channel' number (frequency) and press OK.
  6. Repeat steps 1-5 using the different numbers next to each group
    (a group is a bundle of TV services), to complete the manual retune. D3&4: 25, BBC B HD: 22, SDN: 23, ARQ A: 26, ARQ B: 29, COM7 HD: 32, COM8 HD: 35
  7. Then press 'menu' or 'exit' to finish.
  8. NB: These instructions should work for most Freeview TVs or boxes, but they differ, and you may have to speak to your manufacturer for help.

Will I need a new aerial? Click to expand

To watch Freeview, YouView or BT Vision*, you will need a good aerial of the correct type.

*subscription required.

In some cases it may be necessary to contact a professional aerial installer to fit a new aerial or adjust an existing one. Digital UK recommends viewers look for a Registered Digital Installer or member of another recognised trade body.
